Intelligent Tutoring Systems

We, at the Intelligent Tutoring Systems, like to design, test and use systems to enhance student learning in Tech courses by applying techniques that include video and data mining, artificial intelligence, machine learning, and human-computer interfaces.

Our goal is to develop interactive systems that enhance learning by supporting "digital dialogs" between students and all resources available for a course. The project uses and develops concept mining tools to generate metadata across a diverse set of materials, such as course notes, textbooks, archive of homework questions, databases of tests and solutions, laboratories, web resources, etc. The system adapts to individual skill levels and learning styles to tailor the learning experience to student needs. New technologies, including wireless networks and 3G smart-phones are used to allow anytime, anywhere access to the system.

Since my second semester at Georgia Tech, I have been an undergraduate research assistant under Dr.James H. McClellan working on Intelligent Tutoring System (ITS). I have contributed to the project for the last three years and am making excellent progress by implementing a singular Intelligent Review Model, inspired by Markov Chains, to make the system truly intelligent. By identifying students’ strengths and weaknesses, the model automatically tailors future homework and practice assignments to provide students with comprehensive feedback about their performance and help them improve their conceptual knowledge. The project has been deployed and funded by Georgia Tech and, every semester, is used by more than 250 ECE undergraduates who take Introduction to Digital Signal Processing, ECE 2026.

Due to my experience, I have been fortunate to become Lead Undergraduate Research Assistant and have my own team of undergraduates. Using n-gram analysis, we have designed a question ranking and concepttagging system, which is currently used to tag all new and existing questions of the system. It has led to a 29% increase in tagging efficiency. We have implemented interconnected concept maps and transition matrices, based on cross correlation among different concepts, increasing the overall system prediction of students’ weaknesses by 70%. We have also developed the Intelligent Review Mode, increasing the student participation by 30%. One of the benefits that I enjoy from collaborating with my colleagues is that there ia always a new exciting challenge waiting for me. We are currently in the process of writing a paper on the Intelligent Review Model for submission to upcoming AI and educational conferences.
